Predicting popular YouTube videos among K-12 students

The California-based company GoGuardian helps teachers and school administrators manage chromebooks for millions of K-12 students. These chromebooks represent enormous educational potential, but we all know just how distracting unlimited access to the Internet can be. To further the mission of educators, GoGuardian provides a number of services to filter content and protect students online. Among their more important products is a smart YouTube filter. This is an important feature, because at any hour of the day, approximately 35% of students are watching YouTube[1]. YouTube does host a large amount of valuable educational content, so blocking the entire domain would be overly restrictive. Unfortunately, it hosts even more content that is distracting and not useful in an educational context. To filter the wheat from the chaff, GoGuardian would like to surface a small number of trending videos to administrators and teachers, and enable them to make the decision whether any individual video should be accessible on school-provided laptops. I consulted with them to create the model underlying this dashboard.

Trying to filter all the videos on YouTube at any given time by hand would be impossible. Luckily, if we look into the distribution of students watching different videos over time, a large and fairly constant proportion of students are watching a small number of top videos.

Just 5 of the top 100 videos are being watching by almost ~⅕ of the viewers at any given time, so evaluating those 5 videos would have a large impact with limited effort. Also, by focusing on just the top 5 videos I can smooth out the weekly and daily cycles and focus on just predicting which videos will be popular at any given time. I split out a test set of data from the last two weeks, on which I validated the performance of different prediction algorithms.
